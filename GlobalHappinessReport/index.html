Introduction

The World Happiness Report is a landmark survey of the state of global happiness. The World Happiness 2019, which ranks 155 countries by their happiness levels, uses happiness indicators to inform their policy-making decisions. Leading experts across fields – economics, psychology, survey analysis, national statistics, health, public policy and more – describe how measurements of well-being can be used effectively to assess the progress of nations. The reports review the state of happiness in the world today and show how the new science of happiness explains personal and national variations in happiness.

Top Five Countries

The top five countries that have the most happiness value are Finland, Denmark, Norway, Iceland and Netherlands. With a value of 7.769,7.6,7.554,7.494,7.488 respectively. Surprisingly, the top countries are all located in Europe.

NO.1 FINLAND

ABOUT FINLAND

Finland, officially the Republic of Finland, is a Nordic country in Northern Europe. In 2015, Finland was ranked first in the World Human Capital and the Press Freedom Index and as the most stable country in the world during 2011–2016 in the Fragile States Index, and second in the Global Gender Gap Report.It also ranked first on the World Happiness Report report for 2018, 2019, 2020 and 2021.

______________________________________________________________

NO.2 DENMARK

ABOUT DENMARK

Denmark is a Nordic country in Northern Europe. European Denmark, which is the southernmost of the Scandinavian countries, consists of a peninsula, Jutland, and an archipelago of 443 named islands, with the largest being Zealand, Funen and the North Jutlandic Island. It is the second happiest country in the world in 2019, with a large economic power and a high level of press freedom and a strong social support system.

______________________________________________________________

NO.3 NORWAY

ABOUT NORWAY

Norway, officially the Kingdom of Norway, is a Nordic country in Northern Europe whose mainland territory comprises the western and northernmost portion of the Scandinavian Peninsula. The remote Arctic island of Jan Mayen and the archipelago of Svalbard also form part of Norway. Bouvet Island, located in the Subantarctic, is a dependency of Norway; it also lays claims to the Antarctic territories of Peter I Island and Queen Maud Land. The capital and largest city in Norway is Oslo.
